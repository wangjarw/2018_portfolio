VRVR Golf (3:06)

Role

Videographor

Overview

Goal

Developing a game VR game trailer that showcase the feature of the VR game.

Teammates

Tamiko Tadeo, Kenny Cheung and Liah Castillo

Time

5 days

Other Goals

Creating a casual VR golf game for people to relax themselves and experience futuristic golfing experience in 3.5 months.

Other Roles

sub environmental designer, asset modeller and user researcher.

Brief

This project was assigned in the course, Immersive Environment in which the student had to develop a virtual experience using the VR platform for the audience to feel a sense of high presence and engagement. Within this specific game project, one of my main roles was the videographer so that our VR experience can be documented for promoting VR technology.

Before diving into the videography part, the VR game development would be discussed briefly. This was my team’s first time developing a VR game using Oculus Rift. According to most of our members’ personal VR experiences, a lot of the games were experience based games. For instance, a roller coaster simulator game was played as a start up game for us during the class. Hence, the team thought it might be a good idea to build our game based on something real like a sport and add in more surprising features to excite the players. This was why we decided on the topic of building a golf game.

After our ideation stage, my team wanted to create a golfing experience with the focus of having futuristic but beautifully made environment so that players can be more engaged with the experience. Instead of having to chase after the golf ball being hit, our programmers, Tamiko and Kenny thought it might be interesting to incorporate a teleportation mechanic for players to move around. This decision further minimized the chance of motion sickness of players. Nevertheless, our game strived to have a realistic physics, we conducted playtesting with the users. I made a rough draft of user testing questions and filmed the playtesting process to document the result. Through the process, my team found that due to the controller’s constraint, our players found it hard to map the controller to the real life golfing movement. Hence, I created a tutorial terrain in Unity with basic game mechanics features including teleportation, golf club respawn point and a camera playing the path of the golf ball. Lastly, I was also in charge of modelling some of the in game components in maya including breakable crystal/rocks, cave and golf camera and looking for suitable pre-made models for the players to involve in the game.

Process

At almost the end of the semester, there were only 5 days left for us to film a VR game trailer that demonstrated the overall experience of the VR game we have been doing. I communicate with the team especially the programmers on what they wanted to showcase in the video. They considered it would be the best if the trailer could focus on the game mechanics and dynamics since they felt like showing the smooth physics that could been seen reflection of user feedback. In order to decrease the unpredictability and being able to film quickly, I outlined a simplified version of a shot list and a storyboard for communication between the team mates.

Challenge

However, due to the fact that the school computer that was powerful enough run the VR game could not install any external recording software, the team had to rely on the programmers to record in game footage using their home desktops since they knew the best on how to set up the unity project. I had to make sure they understood the shot list and everything was captured due to the time constraints through constant communication.

Furthermore, on the filming outline, I considered it might be a good idea to feature user experience in the trailer. Hence, I decided to film during the final showcase. The challenge here was that every team member was occupied by other tasks and this meant that I became the only person in charge of filming. It was actually difficult to manage everything at the same time with the presence of our guest players. Nevertheless, in order to minimize the chaos, I planned out beforehand on where to set up the camera and how i was going to film close-up shot to the players. Things turnt out under control during the showcase.

Reflection

At the end, I used Premiere and After Effect to edit and adjust the footage by following the preproduction outline I created. The trailer being produced engaged many other guests and friends who were not able to play our game. The trailer was embedded in our team website and our VR experience was also being introduced on CBC news to promote VR applications. Through this experience, I learnt that time management, communication and pre-production were so important to achieve quality work.
